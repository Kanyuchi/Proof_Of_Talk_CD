**Date:** 2026-05-14

## One-line positioning

**"Find your 5. Not your 500."**

A 30-second hero video for LinkedIn (primary) + X + Instagram Reels (secondary). Hybrid format: AI-cinematic open + real product screens + ElevenLabs voiceover.

## Audience

| Segment | Goal | CTA |
|---|---|---|
| **Existing ticket-holders** (~436) | Activation: claim magic link, complete profile, book meetings | "Check your inbox — your matches are live" |
| **Prospective attendees** | Conversion: position the matchmaker as the reason to buy a ticket | "proofoftalk.io" |

One cut serves both. Existing ticket-holders get an email with their magic link on the same day the video drops (sequenced with email re-enable).

## Why this works

- "Find your 5, not your 500" reframes networking as quality over quantity — the exact pain point our 436 Web3 execs already feel about every conference they've ever attended.
- Real product screens (My Matches, AI Concierge drafting, mutual-match badge, free-slot booking) prove the claim isn't marketing puffery.
- Louvre + $18T stat anchors the premium positioning that justifies the ticket price.

### Production notes
- **Voiceover:** ElevenLabs, warm + measured (NOT hype-bro). Test 2–3 voices, pick the one with the most authority.
- **Music:** Generative orchestral score via Suno, ducking under the VO. No licensed library tracks (every conference app uses the same Artlist beds).
- **AI cinematic shots:** Veo 3 or Sora — Louvre at dusk, particle swarm, Paris drone pull-out. Render multiple takes, pick the most coherent.
- **Product cuts:** Real screen-record from `meet.proofoftalk.io`, faces blurred to honour the privacy-mode promise. Use a real attendee's actual mutual-match card (with their consent — Pouneh Bligaard already engaged on LinkedIn is a candidate).
- **Aspect ratios:** Master at 16:9, export 1:1 (LinkedIn feed) + 9:16 (IG Reels, X mobile) + 16:9 (sales decks).

## Open questions / risks

- **Real-attendee consent for product cuts.** Blurred faces handles privacy, but a single recognisable name in a screen-record is the strongest social proof. Worth asking Pouneh Bligaard or another already-engaged VC if they'd sign a release.
- **AI-cinematic quality drift.** Veo 3 / Sora outputs vary wildly. Block a Saturday for "render until something works" rather than assume the first batch is shippable.
- **LinkedIn algorithm timing.** A Tuesday or Wednesday 09:00 Paris post hits NYC and London prime business hours simultaneously. Avoid Mondays (algorithm noise) and Fridays (low engagement).
