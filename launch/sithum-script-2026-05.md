## Direction notes

| Element | Direction |
|---|---|
| **Tone** | Premium, measured, confident. Louvre Palace energy. NOT hype-bro, NOT corporate. The pace of a Patek Philippe ad, not a SaaS explainer. |
| **VO voice** | ElevenLabs — warm + authoritative. Test Adam, Rachel, Daniel. Pick the one whose silences carry as much weight as the words. |
| **VO pacing** | Slow. Trust the gaps. The brand line at 0:26 needs 4 full seconds to land. |
| **Music** | Generative orchestral (Suno). Soft strings open, low-end percussion enters at 0:05, full lift at 0:22, gentle resolve by 0:28. Around 90 BPM. Ducks under VO at -12 LUFS. |
| **Color grade** | Cinematic shots: warm-dusk Louvre palette. Product cuts: slightly desaturated cool tone so they read as "real" against the cinematic warmth. |
| **On-screen text** | Sans-serif (Inter, Helvetica Neue, or PoT's brand font if one exists). Captions ON for sound-off mobile viewers. Bold for emphasis words only. |
| **Cuts** | No hard cuts on text — 4-6 frame motion blur on every text reveal. Cinematic-to-product transitions should match-cut (light source, motion vector). |

## Beat 1 — Hook (0:00 → 0:05)

**Shot:** Slow cinematic dolly forward toward the Louvre Palace at dusk. Warm window lights flickering on one by one. Pyramid lit from beneath. Subtle atmospheric haze. Shot on 35mm anamorphic feel.

**Source:** Veo 3 / Sora — render 3 variations, pick the one with the cleanest architecture (no warped windows, no melting glass).

**Overlay:** At 0:03, abstract overlay enters — 2,500 small golden dots converging and settling into a loose grid pattern over the Louvre plate. Smooth ease-out motion.

**On-screen text:**
> PROOF OF TALK 2026
> 2–3 June · Louvre Palace

(Bottom-third, fade in over 0:02 → 0:04. Sans-serif, tracked-out, no drop shadow.)

**VO (0:01 → 0:05):**
> *"Two thousand five hundred decision-makers. Eighteen trillion dollars in the room."*

**Delivery note:** Measured. Comma between the two sentences is a full beat — let "eighteen trillion" land.

**Music:** Soft strings only. Music in cold-open from frame 1; build is held back until Beat 2.

**SFX:** Subtle low-frequency rumble under the dolly. Soft "shimmer" whoosh when the golden dots settle (0:03).

## Beat 2 — Tension (0:05 → 0:10)

**Shots (fast cuts, ~1s each):**

1. **0:05 → 0:06** — Macro shot of stacking business cards. Hands placing card on card on card. Top-down. Shallow depth of field. Source: stock (Artgrid) or shoot if Sithum has the time.
2. **0:06 → 0:08** — Generic flooded calendar UI. Wall-to-wall meeting blocks, overlapping invites. Cursor hovering, unable to find a gap. Source: screen-record a stock calendar app, anonymise.
3. **0:08 → 0:10** — Generic conference app UI: an endless scroll of attendee cards, no clear way in. Source: screen-record a competitor app (Brella / Whova) — keep faces blurred to avoid clearance issues.

**On-screen text (0:07 → 0:09):**
> 500?

Large, centred, strikes through with a single horizontal line at 0:09. Disappears by 0:10.

**VO (0:05 → 0:09):**
> *"You could meet five hundred of them…"*

**Delivery note:** Trails off on "of them" — the ellipsis is real. Hold the silence for 1 second before Beat 3 opens.

**Music:** Low-end percussion enters at 0:05. Tension hit on the strikethrough at 0:09.

**SFX:** Subtle "card slap" on each business-card cut. Cursor click on the calendar (0:07). Sharp "strike" on the strikethrough (0:09).

## Beat 3 — Product Proof (0:10 → 0:22)

This is the heart of the video. Sithum has all five product screen-records (delivered separately). Sequence them in this order with quick, confident cuts. No screen sits longer than 3 seconds.

**Shot 1 — My Matches (0:10 → 0:13):**
- Land on the My Matches page on `meet.proofoftalk.io`.
- Slow scroll through 3 mutual-match cards.
- Highlight the one-line "why this matters" reason under each name (callout box with subtle pulse — guides the eye).

**Shot 2 — AI Concierge drafting (0:13 → 0:17):**
- Open the AI Concierge chat panel.
- *"Your profile is 67% complete — I can draft your conference goals based on your role and profile."*
- User taps **"Yes, draft my goals."**
- Three candidate chips fade in.
- User taps the middle one → textarea opens pre-filled → user edits one word → taps Save.
- Green confirmation: *"Saved. I've kicked off a match refresh in the background."*

This shot proves the AI is doing real work, not theatre. Sithum: keep this beat 4 seconds — it's the longest single shot in the video.

**Shot 3 — Mutual match alert (0:17 → 0:19):**
- Land on home page with the orange "Mutual interest" nav badge visible.
- Tap through → land on the mutual-match card with the named attendee (consented — use the real signed-release name from Task 2 of the production plan, or blur if no release).
- *"Marcus also picked you."*

**Shot 4 — Free-slot booking (0:19 → 0:22):**
- Show the booked meeting card on My Matches with a real date + time + location chip.
- The chip reads: *"Both free at 11:30 — Tue 2 June, Café Marly."*

**On-screen text (sticky lower-third throughout Beat 3):**
> Find your 5

Small, lower-left. Doesn't compete with the product UI.

**VO (0:10 → 0:21):**
> *"…or let our AI read every profile, find the five who matter to you, and book the meeting."*

**Delivery note:** Pace picks up here. This is the "answer" to Beat 2's question. Each clause lands on a product screen — "read every profile" on Shot 1, "find the five" on Shot 2, "book the meeting" on Shot 4. Sync the VO edit to the cuts, not the other way around.

**Music:** Orchestral lift starts gently at 0:12 and builds toward Beat 4. No big swell here — that's for Beat 4.

**SFX:** Soft UI tap when "Yes, draft my goals" is pressed (0:14). Subtle "save" chime on the green confirmation (0:16). The "Marcus also picked you" badge gets a soft notification ping (0:17).

> Not your 500.

Then at 0:28, the URL replaces the brand line in the lower-third:

**Music:** Full orchestral lift at 0:22 → peak at 0:26 over the brand line → gentle resolve by 0:30.

**SFX:** Soft "logo land" thump as the POT logo lands at 0:27. Nothing else — the music carries the close.

## Hard rules (don't break)

1. **Brand line must close every cut.** No variants. *"Find your 5. Not your 500."*
2. **No licensed music library tracks.** Suno generative or original. Every conference app uses the same Artlist beds — sounds cheap.
3. **No AI-obvious humans** in the cinematic shots. People in the distance only. No melting faces, no warped limbs.
4. **Product cuts must use real data.** Real attendee names (consented), real timestamps, real verticals. No `John Doe` / `Lorem Ipsum`.
5. **30 seconds, hard-capped.** If a beat overruns, trim the beat. Don't extend the runtime.
6. **No voiceover puns on "five" / "5"** beyond the brand line. The closing line is the only place that pivot works — overusing it dilutes it.
